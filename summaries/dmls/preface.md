### **Core idea:**

**ML systems are both complex and unique — and this book exists to give you the nuanced, system-level framework that blog posts and tutorials can't.**

Put simply: **understanding the whole system — not just the algorithm — is what separates ML engineers who ship from those who stall.**

---

### **The central principle**

Every question about ML in production has the same honest answer: *"It depends."* What it depends on is the entire stack — the data, the business requirements, the infrastructure, the stakeholders, and the constraints. This book is the long-form version of that answer.

---

### **What makes this book different**

Most ML resources focus on one piece of the puzzle:

- Blog posts cover a single technique in isolation
- Courses optimize for model accuracy on clean datasets
- Tutorials assume one model, one team, one use case

This book takes a **holistic, systems approach** — treating an ML system as a collection of tightly coupled components where changing one affects all others.

---

### **Who it's for**

- ML engineers and data scientists bringing models to production
- Data engineers building the pipelines that feed those models
- Engineering managers and platform engineers owning the infrastructure
- Technical leaders evaluating whether to adopt ML solutions

The book assumes basic ML knowledge (gradient descent, loss functions, classification vs. regression) but focuses entirely on the *practical* side: deployment, monitoring, data quality, fairness, team structure.

---

### **What it is not**

Not an introduction to ML algorithms. Not a tutorial book with step-by-step code. Not a guide to any single tool. Instead: a framework for **evaluating trade-offs** that will outlast any specific technology.

---

### **The book's structure**

- **Chapters 1–2**: Foundations — when to use ML, how to frame the problem, what requirements matter
- **Chapter 3**: Data engineering fundamentals
- **Chapters 4–6**: Pre-deployment — training data, feature engineering, model development
- **Chapters 7–9**: Deployment and post-deployment — serving predictions, monitoring, continual updates
- **Chapter 10**: Infrastructure and MLOps tooling
- **Chapter 11**: The human side — user experience, team structure, responsible AI

---

### **The chapter's principle**

> ML systems are complex because they consist of many interdependent components, and unique because they are data-dependent — requiring a holistic framework, not isolated answers.

---

### **Bottom line**

If you want to:

- **Stop being surprised when your model degrades in production**

Then:

- **Stop thinking of ML as "train a model, ship it, done."**
- Start thinking in systems: data pipelines, monitoring, retraining cycles, stakeholder alignment.

**The algorithm is the smallest part of the problem. The system is the real challenge.**
