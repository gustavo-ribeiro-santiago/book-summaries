# Chapter 8: Secrets — Zero to One

## Overview

What secrets are left to discover? Thiel argues that the world is full of secrets — important truths that are unknown or ignored by most people — and that every great business is built on one. The willingness to seek and believe in secrets is what separates transformative entrepreneurs from everyone else.

## Key Concepts

- **The Spectrum from Convention to Mystery**:
  - *Conventions*: Things everyone knows (Pythagorean theorem, boiling point of water). Widely shared, no edge.
  - *Secrets*: Things that are true but not yet widely known. Hard but discoverable.
  - *Mysteries*: Things that may be impossible to know (string theory, certain metaphysics). Not worth pursuing.
  - The key insight: secrets are neither conventions nor mysteries. They are hard to find but findable.
- **Why People Have Stopped Looking**:
  1. *Incrementalism*: Education rewards small, measurable steps — not leaps into the unknown.
  2. *Risk aversion*: Seeking a secret means risking being lonely and wrong.
  3. *Complacency*: Social elites have the most freedom to explore new thinking but believe in secrets the least. Why search if you're already comfortable?
  4. *Flatness*: If the world is a single competitive marketplace, surely smarter people have already found it?
- **The Unabomber as Extreme Example**: Ted Kaczynski believed there were no secrets left — no meaningful problems that individuals could solve. His nihilism drove him to violence. Thiel uses this as a dark illustration of what happens when belief in secrets collapses entirely.
- **A World Without Secrets Is Wrong**: Financial bubbles demonstrate that markets are not perfectly efficient — people collectively believe something false. Injustices persist because most people don't recognize them as secrets. Airbnb and Uber both found secrets: that ordinary people had underutilized assets and that drivers' time was undeployed. These weren't obvious — they were hidden.
- **Natural vs. Human Secrets**:
  - *Natural secrets*: Things about the physical world not yet discovered (new physics, chemistry, biology).
  - *Human secrets*: Things about people and society that are not widely acknowledged (what people don't say in public, what they need but won't admit).
- **What to Do with Secrets**: Share them selectively. Tell those you need to build with. "A company is essentially a conspiracy to change the world." The best companies are built around a specific secret — a hidden truth about what the world needs that most people haven't seen yet.

## Core Message

Every great company answers a contrarian question: what valuable company is nobody building? Every correct answer is a secret — something important, true, and largely unknown. The founders of transformative companies don't just work hard; they believe that the world still holds secrets worth finding and that they are capable of finding them.
