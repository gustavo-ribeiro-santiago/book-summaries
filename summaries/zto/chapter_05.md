# Chapter 5: Last Mover Advantage — Zero to One

## Overview

This chapter argues that a startup's value lies overwhelmingly in its future cash flows, not its present performance — and that durable monopoly requires specific characteristics. The "last mover advantage" is more important than being first: what matters is building a business that dominates its market for years to come.

## Key Concepts

- **Value = Future Cash Flows**: Twitter at its IPO was worth 12x the New York Times despite losing money, while the Times was profitable. The reason is cash flows: the Times's monopoly days are over, while Twitter's investors expected durable monopoly profits for a decade ahead. A company's value today is the discounted sum of all the money it will make in the future.
- **Technology Companies Are Back-Loaded**: Unlike Old Economy businesses whose value lies in the near term, tech companies often lose money for years while building something valuable — then earn most of their value over a distant horizon. PayPal in March 2000 (two years old, not yet profitable) had 75% of its present value in profits from 2011 and beyond.
- **Four Characteristics of Durable Monopoly**:
  1. *Proprietary Technology*: Must be 10x better than the closest substitute in some important dimension. Anything less is just a marginal improvement and easily copied.
  2. *Network Effects*: The product becomes more valuable as more people use it (e.g., Facebook). But must start valuable even for the first user — otherwise you'll never reach critical mass.
  3. *Economies of Scale*: Fixed costs spread over more users; marginal cost of serving another customer is near zero. Software businesses scale extremely well.
  4. *Branding*: Strong identity creates lasting value, but branding alone (without substance) is fragile.
- **Building a Monopoly: Start Small and Dominate**:
  - Start with a very small market and dominate it completely before expanding.
  - Amazon started with books (not all retail), Facebook started with Harvard students (not all social networking), PayPal started with eBay PowerSellers.
  - Disruption is a tactical concept, not a goal — don't define yourself by opposition to incumbents.
- **Last Mover Advantage**: It's better to be the *last* great development in a market — to make the final major improvement and enjoy years of monopoly profits — than to be first in a market that others quickly commoditize. The goal is durable dominance, not early arrival.

## Core Message

Entrepreneurs are often seduced by short-term metrics (weekly active users, quarterly earnings) while ignoring what actually creates value: durable, compounding cash flows from a dominant position. Start small, dominate completely, then expand — and always ask whether you're building something that will be the *last* great mover in a market, not just the first.
