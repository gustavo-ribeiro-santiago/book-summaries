# Prefaces — Getting to Yes

## Overview

The prefaces to the three editions of *Getting to Yes* trace the book's remarkable journey from a slim, practical guide to a global reference point for collaborative negotiation. Thirty years after its first publication, the authors reflect on a quiet but profound "negotiation revolution" that has transformed how people in families, organizations, and nations make decisions together.

## Key Concepts

- **The Negotiation Revolution**: A generation ago, decision-making was hierarchical—those at the top commanded and others obeyed. Today, flat organizations, fast innovation, and networked communication mean almost everyone must negotiate constantly, with people and organizations over whom they have no direct control.
- **From Adversarial to Collaborative**: The old view of negotiation as a contest where someone must "give in" has given way to a growing recognition that both sides can benefit—that "win-win" solutions are possible and that wise agreements can emerge even when full mutual gain is elusive.
- **Conflict as a Growth Industry**: The authors note that democratization and flatter hierarchies surface conflict rather than suppress it. The goal is not to eliminate conflict—which is often the driver of change, justice, and prosperity—but to transform how we deal with it: from destructive battling to hard-headed, side-by-side problem-solving.
- **Refinements Over Editions**: The second edition added "Ten Questions People Ask About Getting to YES," addressing topics like dealing with irrational parties, tactical questions, and the role of power. The third edition updated examples, clarified key concepts (notably that "separate the people from the problem" means *disentangle*, not *ignore* personal dynamics), and added guidance on digital communication's impact on negotiations.
- **Soft on People, Hard on Problem**: A persistent theme of clarification across editions is that principled negotiation means being respectful of people while being rigorous about substance—not sacrificing one for the other.

## Core Message

The prefaces frame the book as a timely and timeless tool: negotiation is now a universal skill, conflict is inevitable and even valuable, and the world urgently needs better methods for transforming disagreement into wise, durable agreements. *Getting to Yes* offers a principled, practical path toward that goal.
