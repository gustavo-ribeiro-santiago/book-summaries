### **Core idea:**

**Make the other person happy about doing what you suggest.**

Put simply: **frame requests so they benefit the other person.**

---

### **The central principle**

People do things willingly when they see a benefit for themselves.

Instead of commanding or demanding, show how your request serves their interests.

> Make the other person happy about doing the thing you suggest.

---

### **Why this works**

- It transforms obligation into opportunity
- It creates willing cooperation
- It preserves the relationship
- It often leads to better results

A person who wants to do something does it better than one who has to.

---

### **How to apply this**

Before making a request, ask:
- "What's in it for them?"
- "How can I frame this as beneficial?"
- "What do they care about?"

Then present your request in those terms.

---

### **Techniques for creating happiness**

1. Be sincere—don't promise what you can't deliver
2. Know exactly what you want them to do
3. Be empathetic—consider their needs
4. Match benefits to their wants
5. Make the request in terms of their benefit

---

### **Example**

Instead of: "I need you to clean the stockroom."

Try: "If you could organize the stockroom, it would look great for the inspection, and you won't have to deal with it later when things are busier."

---

### **The leadership application**

Effective leaders don't just give orders—they:
- Give titles and authority
- Create opportunities for recognition
- Connect tasks to personal goals
- Make people feel valued

---

### **The chapter's principle**

> PRINCIPLE 9: Make the other person happy about doing the thing you suggest.

---

### **Bottom line**

If you want to:
- Get enthusiastic cooperation
- Lead without creating resentment
- Build willing teams

Then:
- Find the benefit for them
- Frame requests in their terms
- Make compliance feel like opportunity

**People gladly do what serves their interests.**
