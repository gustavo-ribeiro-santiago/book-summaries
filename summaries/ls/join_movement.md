# Chapter 14: Join the Movement

## A Global Community

The Lean Startup is not just a book — it is an ongoing global movement. Ries closes with a curated list of resources for entrepreneurs who want to go deeper and connect with others practicing these ideas.

## Key Resources

- **Lean Startup Meetups**: Over 100 organized local groups worldwide (San Francisco, Boston, New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, and more). Find groups at lean-startup.meetup.com.
- **Lean Startup Circle**: A large mailing list community founded by Rich Collins, with thousands of entrepreneurs sharing questions and stories daily at leanstartupcircle.com.
- **Lean Startup Wiki**: A comprehensive list of events and resources maintained by volunteers.
- **Startup Lessons Learned Conference**: Annual conference organized by Ries.

## Essential Reading

- **Steve Blank**, *The Four Steps to the Epiphany*: The foundational book on Customer Development, a direct predecessor and companion to the Lean Startup.
- **Clayton Christensen**, *The Innovator's Dilemma* and *The Innovator's Solution*: The definitive work on disruptive innovation.
- **Geoffrey Moore**, *Crossing the Chasm*: The classic guide to winning mainstream customers after early adopters.
- **Taiichi Ohno**, *Toyota Production System*: The source of lean manufacturing, from which the Lean Startup draws directly.
- **Donald Reinertsen**, *The Principles of Product Development Flow*: Deep analysis of batch size and WIP in product development.
- **W. Edwards Deming**, *Out of the Crisis*: The systems-thinking foundation for quality and continuous improvement.

## Key Takeaway

> "Reading is good, action is better."

The most important resources are local — other entrepreneurs wrestling with the same challenges. Join a community, run your first experiment, and start learning.
