# Chapter 2: Define

## Who Is an Entrepreneur?

Ries broadens the definition of entrepreneur far beyond the garage-startup archetype. Anyone who creates a new product or service **under conditions of extreme uncertainty** is an entrepreneur — whether in a VC-backed startup, a Fortune 500 division, a nonprofit, or a government agency.

**A startup** is formally defined as:
> "A human institution designed to create a new product or service under conditions of extreme uncertainty."

The key word is *uncertainty*. A franchise opening an exact clone of an existing business is not a startup — its success depends purely on execution. A startup, by contrast, doesn't yet know who its customer is or whether its product will work.

## Intrapreneurs and the Enterprise

Ries illustrates with Intuit's **SnapTax** — a mobile tax-filing app built by a small internal team at one of America's largest finance software companies. The team had scarce resources, an "island of freedom" from management, and ran rapid experiments. The result was a breakthrough product that competed directly with Intuit's own flagship.

The lesson: innovation is not just for garages. Large companies like Intuit can cultivate entrepreneurship at scale if they build the right management culture.

## A 7,000-Person Lean Startup

Scott Cook (Intuit's founder) recognized that too many internal products were failing despite rigorous traditional management. He concluded the prevailing management paradigm was inadequate for continuous innovation. His solution: treat every division as a startup, run hundreds of small experiments instead of one big bet, and measure how much revenue comes from products that didn't exist three years ago.

Intuit's TurboTax team went from one major annual initiative to running **500+ tests per tax season** — weekly cycles of build, measure, learn. The result: more learning, more entrepreneurs, fewer internal politicians.

## Why This Matters

Senior management must *create the conditions* for entrepreneurship — the systems, platforms, and culture that allow teams to experiment. The goal is not to find brilliant individuals, but to build a machine that converts ideas into validated learning reliably.

## Key Takeaway

> "Entrepreneurship is management — a startup is an institution, not just a product."

The Lean Startup is designed for entrepreneurs everywhere, inside and outside traditional organizations.
